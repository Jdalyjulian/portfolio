Take a look at what my students are learning

Biosphere Bottles

Self-sustained ecosystems inside 2 liter bottles. 15 bottles were created and after 5 weeks, 4 bottles have successfully maintained a balanced ecosystem. Some have even gained new life with the addition of new snails.

Watch Video

Case-Based Learning

Case-based learning is a method of eliciting what students already know and guiding them towards applying this knowledge to solve a puzzle. Along the way, students determine what information is missing and participate in mini-lessons to fill in the gaps.

Watch Video

Plant Mazes

Students test plant stimulus-response scenarios by placing a potted seed inside a box. The box has s hole cut in the top, with obstacles between the pot and the hole. The plant grows towards the light and around the obstacles. A process called phototropism.

Watch Video

Gamify Learning

Students enjoy a game factor. To understand how matter is cycled in an ecosystem, students play trophic level tag, a scientific tag involving the roles of producers, primary consumers, secondary consumers, tertiary consumers, and decomposers.
